Restaurants

The Lower East Side is famous for its unique cultural mix. As a result, the Lower East Side has become the center of attention for people who want to explore new flavors activites. I have compiled a list of my favorite places get food in this area. Hopefully this inspires your adventures.

My Favorite Places to Eat in the L.E.S.

  • North Dumplings (Essex)

    North Dumpling offers a good selection of food, and, most famously, dumplings. They have the best dumplings I have ever had in my life, and the public believes the same as recently they moved to a larger location on the same street.

  • Sqaure Pizza (Clinton)

    This place has one of the best gourmet slices I have had. The staff is very nice, and friendly.

  • Hawa Smoothies & Bubble Tea (Delancey)

    There is a wide variety of beverages you can get here, and a great amount of customization. This is a great meetup spot for friends before going on an adventure.

  • DupBopBro - Korean Rice Bowl (E. Houston)

    This a great place for Korean food. The staff is super friendly. You can fully customize your bowl, and there are many options. Finding the best combination may take time but it is definitely worth it as you experiences new flavors in each visit.

  • Makabely Pizza (Columbia)

    Makabely is a location I have been going to since I was a kid. They sell good slices, and a good atmosphere filled with music. They serve not only pizza, but latin cuisine, so they have a wide selection. They have a wall of photographs from the founder of the chain with celebrities that is always interesting to look at. A fun game to play while waiting for your food is seeing how many celebrities you can name on the wall.

  • Katz's Delicatessen (E. Houston)

    This is most likely the most famous place here. It is incredibly well known for their sandwiches, meat, and Jewish cuisine. This is a hotspot for tourists, and lines can reach halfway around the block.
